- Reduced time-to-market for brand migrations by 60%

- Cut design and engineering duplication costs by £2M annually

**My role:** Lead Product Designer. I defined the UX architecture, led the cross-functional task force, built the design system from scratch, and secured C-suite buy-in for the "clean sheet" approach across 40+ brand stakeholders.

---

## The problem we were solving

The organisation ran 40+ brand websites built for mixed B2B and B2C needs.

This created:

- Fragmented customer journeys with 300%+ variation in conversion rates
- Inconsistent quality and duplicated effort across brands
- 18-month lead times for new features
- Poor performance, accessibility, and SEO outcomes

PO1 was created to fix this at a system level.

The goal was not to redesign a website. It was to build a repeatable B2C ecommerce capability that could scale.

---

## Programme intent

PO1 set out to do three things:

1. Move the business to an ecommerce-first model
2. Create one consolidated platform and design system
3. Enable faster, data-led iteration across brands

This required changes to technology, design practice, team structure, and governance. The programme was brand-agnostic by design.

**My leadership challenge:** The "clean sheet" principle was the hardest sell. It required navigating 10+ years of legacy pride from individual brand stakeholders. I used analytics audits to prove that legacy patterns were actively costing the business revenue, which shifted the conversation from opinion to evidence.

---

## Guiding principles

I established five principles that shaped every decision:

- Start from a clean sheet
- Design for B2B only
- Optimise for performance and conversion first
- Validate assumptions early with real users
- Build once, reuse everywhere

This avoided incremental improvement of legacy patterns.

---

## Programme structure

I helped establish PO1 as a standalone programme with clear governance.

**Governance:**

- Executive steering group for direction and prioritisation
- Clear ownership and decision rights
- Regular show-and-tell checkpoints

This reduced drift and blocked local optimisation.

**Team model:**

- Central, cross-functional task force
- Product, design, tech, data, CRM, marketing, operations
- One team operating as a single delivery unit

I bridged the gap between the brand CEOs and the tech task force, ensuring that "brand-agnostic" didn't mean "brand-less." This secured buy-in for a shared component library while preserving each brand's identity layer.

This replaced brand-by-brand execution and cut delivery time by more than half.

---

## Strategic pillars

I structured the work around five connected pillars, each with clear success metrics.

**1. B2C growth**

Shift from brochure-led sites to conversion-led ecommerce journeys.

Focus areas:

- Entry points (improved findability by 28%)
- Product discovery (reduced bounce rate by 15%)
- Booking confidence (increased checkout completion by 12%)

**2. Customer-centric model**

Design end-to-end journeys from first visit to return.

I mapped the complete journey across: Acquisition, Browsing, Booking, Post-booking management.

This revealed three critical drop-off points that had been invisible when analysing brands separately.

**3. Data-driven decisions**

I established data as the starting point for all design decisions.

Inputs included: Analytics audits across all 40+ brands, conversion data and behavioural patterns, user research and usability testing.

Data framed the problem before design started.

**4. Personalised experiences**

Improve relevance without adding complexity.

I designed: Recently viewed content systems, contextual recommendations, smarter reuse of high-intent pages.

These features increased return visit conversion by 18%.

**5. Operating model**

Reduce duplication and cost by centralising capability. One platform. One design system. One delivery model.

This saved £2M annually in duplicated design and engineering effort.

## Discovery and validation approach

I treated every assumption as a risk.

Key methods:

- Analytics audits across all brands
- Performance and accessibility benchmarking
- Remote design sprints
- Rapid prototyping
- Continuous user testing with 5-8 users per cycle

Small samples were used deliberately. Speed mattered more than scale at this stage.

**Result:** We validated 12 major design decisions in 8 weeks, compared to the previous 6-month waterfall cycle.

---

## Design approach

I positioned design as a system, not a surface.

Key decisions:

- Progressive web app principles blended with traditional web
- Fewer components (40 vs 200+), used more consistently
- Shorter scroll and clearer information hierarchy
- Performance and accessibility treated as design inputs, not afterthoughts

I established a weekly design-data-engineering sync that became the primary decision-making forum.

**Outcome:** Page load times improved by 40%. Accessibility compliance went from 30% to 95% WCAG 2.1 AA.

---

## Building the design system

I built the multi-brand design system from scratch.

**The challenge:** Create one system that could serve 40+ brands without destroying their individual identities.

**My approach:**

- Separated structure from style using a token architecture
- Created a core component library (40 components)
- Built brand-specific theme layers
- Established clear governance and contribution models

**The system included:**

- Design tokens for colour, typography, spacing, and motion
- Component library in Figma and Storybook
- Documentation and usage guidelines
- Handoff standards and QA processes

**Result:** New brand migrations dropped from 18 months to 6 weeks. Design-to-development handoff time reduced by 70%.

---

## Scope control

I authored the formal UX scope document that served as the contract between design and engineering.

It defined: MVP features and success metrics, templates and components, integrations and dependencies, non-functional requirements, and what was explicitly out of scope.

This prevented feature creep from local brand offices and kept the MVP delivery on its 6-month track.
